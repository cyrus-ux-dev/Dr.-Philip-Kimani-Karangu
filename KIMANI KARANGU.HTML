About Nyandarua County

Nyandarua County sits in the central highlands of Kenya, nestled against the Aberdare Range. It is one of Kenya's most agriculturally rich counties, a land of rolling green plateaus, highland forests, dairy farms, and potato fields that feed the nation. The people of Nyandarua — hardworking, resilient, and deeply rooted in community — deserve leadership that matches their spirit.

~721K Estimated population (2025)
5 Constituencies: Kinangop, Kipipiri, Ol Kalou, Ol Jorok, Ndaragwa
25 Electoral wards across the county
3,286 km² Total geographical area

County Profile

The Heart of Nyandarua

Agricultural Heritage

Nyandarua is Kenya's leading producer of Irish potatoes, cabbages, and dairy products. The county feeds millions of Kenyans every day. Yet our farmers still face poor roads, market volatility, and crop disease. Kimani's plan is to modernise agricultural infrastructure and establish farmer cooperatives that guarantee fair prices.

Natural Resources

The Aberdare Range — rising to 3,999 metres — is the county's most iconic natural landmark, a critical water tower for the nation and a draw for local and international tourism. Protecting this ecosystem while creating sustainable livelihoods around it is a core pillar of the agenda.

Youth & Sports Legacy

Nyandarua is home to world-class athletes including Catherine Ndereba, John Ngugi, and the late Samuel Wanjiru. The county's talent is unmatched — but requires investment in sports facilities, youth programs, and vocational training to unlock its full potential.

Education & Opportunity

Nyandarua now has a university college under Dedan Kimathi University, but more must be done. The agenda will push for expanded technical colleges, scholarship programs for bright students from low-income families, and digital learning infrastructure across all 5 constituencies.

Healthcare Access

County hospitals in Ol Kalou, Engineer, and Nyahururu serve thousands of residents. Kimani's administration will prioritise equipping health facilities with modern supplies, hiring qualified medical staff, and ensuring no Nyandarua resident is turned away from care due to shortage of resources.

Economic Growth

Nyandarua's economy is the 17th largest in Kenya with a GDP of $4.6B at PPP, and is among the 5 richest counties by per capita income. With the right leadership, this foundation can be transformed into inclusive prosperity — better roads, reliable water, and fair opportunities for every resident.

Voter Registration & Electoral Information

The Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) requires all eligible Kenyan citizens to register as voters before participating in elections. Your vote is the foundation of change in Nyandarua. Registration requires a valid Kenyan National ID card or Kenyan passport and is conducted at designated IEBC registration centres across the county.

To verify your voter registration status, visit:

https://verify.iebc.or.ke/ ↗
IEBC Official Website ↗

Registration Requirements

  • Valid Kenyan National ID or Kenyan Passport
  • Must be 18 years of age or older
  • Register at your nearest IEBC centre in Nyandarua
  • Ensure your details are correctly captured
  • Confirm your ward and polling station
